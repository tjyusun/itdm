Definition 4.3.5. Multiplicative Inverse.

Given natural numbers \(a, m\) such that \(\gcd(a,m) = 1\text{,}\) the number \(b\) is called a multiplicative inverse of \(a\) modulo \(m\) if

\begin{equation*} ab \equiv 1 \Mod{m}\text{.} \end{equation*}

We can denote this inverse as \(a^{-1} = b\text{,}\) or by \(a^{-1} \Mod{m}\) to make the modulus explicit.
