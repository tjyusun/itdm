Let \(S = \{6,8,10,12,\ldots,30,32\}\text{.}\)

  1. Show that if seven distinct integers are picked from \(S\text{,}\) that two of these integers will have a sum that is divisible by three.
  2. How many distinct integers do you need to pick from \(S\) to guarantee that three of them sum of a multiple of three?
