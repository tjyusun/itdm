We can generalize CheckpointÂ 2.1.3as follows: if \(n\) people want to sit in a row of \(n\) seats (with no restriction), then the whole operation of assigning seats to people takes \(n\) steps, where the first person has \(n\) seats to choose from, the next person has \(n-1\text{,}\) and so on, until 1 seat is left for the last person. The Product Rule then gives a total number of

\begin{equation*} n \cdot (n-1) \cdot (n-2) \cdot \ldots \cdot 2 \cdot 1 \end{equation*}

ways that \(n\) people can sit in \(n\) seats in a row.
