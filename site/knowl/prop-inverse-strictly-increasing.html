Proposition A.2.5. Inverse of Strictly Increasing is Strictly Increasing.

Let \(A\text{,}\) \(B \subseteq \mathbb{R}\text{,}\) and let \(f:A \rightarrow B\) be a bijective function. If \(f\) is strictly increasing on \(A\text{,}\) then \(f^{-1}\) is strictly increasing on \(B\text{.}\)
