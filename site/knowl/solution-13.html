b. Take \(x = a\) and \(y = -b\) with \(n = 5\) in Theorem 2.3.5. The term \(a^2b^3\) corresponds to the index \(k = 3\text{,}\) which is

\begin{equation*} \binom{5}{3}a^{5-3}(-b)^3 = -\binom{5}{3}a^2b^3\text{,} \end{equation*}

and so its coefficient is \(-\displaystyle\binom{5}{3} = -10\text{.}\)

c. Take \(x = 3a\) and \(y=2b\) with \(n = 5\) in Theorem 2.3.5. The term \(a^2b^3\) corresponds to the index \(k = 3\text{,}\) which is

\begin{equation*} \binom{5}{3}(3a)^{5-3}(2b)^3 = \binom{5}{3}(72a^2b^3)\text{,} \end{equation*}

so its coefficient is \(\displaystyle\binom{5}{3}(72) = 720\text{.}\)
