Let \(m_1,m_2,\ldots,m_n \in \mathbb{N}\) and \(\gcd(m_i,m_j) = 1\) for \(i \ne j\text{,}\) and consider the system

\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} x \equiv a_1 \Mod{m_1} \\ x \equiv a_2 \Mod{m_2} \\ \vdots \\ x \equiv a_n \Mod{m_n} \end{cases} \end{equation*}

Perform the following steps:

  • Let \(M = m_1m_2\cdots m_n\text{.}\)

  • For each \(i = 1,2,\ldots,n\text{:}\)

    • Define \(b_i = M/m_i\) (the product of all moduli other than \(m_i\)).
    • Find the multiplicative inverse of \(b_i\) modulo \(m_i\text{,}\) call it \(t_i\text{.}\)

A solution to the given system is

\begin{equation*} x \equiv a_1b_1t_1 + a_2b_2t_2 + \cdots + a_nb_nt_n \Mod{M}\equiv \sum_{i=1}^n a_ib_it_i \Mod{M}\text{,} \end{equation*}

and this is unique modulo \(M\text{.}\)
