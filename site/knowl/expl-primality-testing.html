Exploration 4.4.1. Primality Testing.

Theorem 4.4.10 asserts that if \(p\) is prime and \(\gcd(a,p)=1\text{,}\) then \(a^{p-1} \equiv 1 \Mod{p}\text{.}\)

(a)

Note that the converse of this statement is not true in general: Even if \(\gcd(a,m) = 1\) and \(a^{m-1} \equiv 1 \Mod{m}\text{,}\) we would not be able to conclude that \(m\) is prime.

Can you give examples of pairs \(a, m\) such that \(\gcd(a,m) = 1\) and \(a^{m-1} \equiv 1 \Mod{m}\) are both true, but \(m\) is not prime.

(b)

Complete the contrapositive of Theorem 4.4.10:

(c)

Show that 91 is not prime by using part (b) with \(a = 2\text{.}\)
