It is a partition of the collection of all \(n\)-subsets to divide them into cases by how many of the \(a_i\)'s are picked, from \(k = 0\) to \(k=n\text{.}\) Hence the sum rule gives the left-hand side:

\begin{equation*} \binom{n}{0}\binom{n}{0} + \binom{n}{1}\binom{n}{1} + \cdots + \binom{n}{n}\binom{n}{n} = \sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k}^2\text{.} \end{equation*}

Therefore the equation

\begin{equation*} \sum_{k=0}^n \binom{n}{k}^2 = \binom{2n}{n} \end{equation*}

holds.
